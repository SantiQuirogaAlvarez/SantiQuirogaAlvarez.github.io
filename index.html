About

Santiago Quiroga is an undergraduate student in the Data Science program at the University of Colorado at Boulder. He bridges theoretical problem-solving in multivariable calculus, physics, and statics with practical project experience and technical proficiency in C, assembly, and web technologies. As a Certified SolidWorks Associate, he brings hands-on engineering project experience; he worked to convert a Chevrolet S-10 pickup into an electric vehicle.

He has applied his skills in diverse contexts. At Skyryse Incorporated, he served as a research engineering intern, exploring 3D airspace visualization for SkyOS, a universal fly-by-wire system. At Cabin, an eco-friendly prefabricated housing company in Mexico, he improved website performance by boosting SEO rankings and cutting loading times by 50% through a full site redesign and English translation. He also designed a prototype cabin with Internet of Things integration for remote management and authored a study on Valle de Bravo's urban planning challenges, combining analytics and expert interviews to identify critical errors and recommend strategies for sustainable growth.
